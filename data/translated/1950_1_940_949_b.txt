All the accused have been accused of being members of the Communist Party with the policy of overthrowing the present government of Hyderabad by violence and establishing a communist regime in its place. It is alleged that they had demanded donations for their communist organization and a few villagers who did not comply with their demands were taken away and killed on September 21, 1948. They were charged with various offenses, including murder, before a special tribunal set up under the rules introduced by the ... military governor under the authority of the Nizam, and were convicted and sentenced to death on August 8, 9 and 14, 1949, through separate verdicts. The petitioners appealed from those verdicts to the High Court of Hyderabad, and the High Court, by its judgments dated December 12, 13 and 14, 1949 respectively, dismissed the appeals. The applicants applied to the High court on January 21, 1950, for a certificate to appeal to the Judiciary Committee of the State of Hyderabad. It appears that ... The Nizam issued a statement on November 23, 1949, stating that the proposed Constitution was suitable for the Government of Hyderabad, India, and that he had approved it as the Constitution of the state of Hyderabad, one of the states in Part B of the First Schedule. On January 26, 1950, the Constitution of India applied to the Union of India and the States in Part B. The petitions originally filed for the certificate of appeal to the Jeudicial Committee of the Privy Council of the States of Hyderabad, with the permission of the Court, were modified and became petitions under Article 134 of the Constitution Of India. A Division Bench of the High Court at Hyderabad considered the petitions and dismissed them on the ground that no such petition was under Article 136 and they further stated that no case was made for a certification on the merits as sought by the petitioners. The petitioner has now filed his petitions before this Court under Article 141 of the Constitution of England, from the judgments of the High court dated December 8, 13 and 4, 1949, for special leave to appeal.

(b) the areas specified in Part D of the First Schedule, (e.g., the Andaman and Nicobar Islands) and
